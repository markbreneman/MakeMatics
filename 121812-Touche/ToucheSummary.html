So What's This Paper All About?

So What's This Paper All About

In the most concise terms this paper is about a new touch sensing technology called Touché...

Touché is a novel capacitive touch sensing technology that provides rich touch and gesture sensitivity to a variety of analogue and digital objects.

Ok So Hows it Work?

So Hows it Work?

Touché is not necessarily new technology, but rather a new implementation of an existing technology...capacitive touch

So Hows it Work?

In typical capacitive touch system, a conductive object is excited by an electrical signal at a fixed frequency. With the Touché technology, a variety of frequencies are monitored.

So Hows it Work?

By monitoring the capacitive response over a range of frequencies, and using machine learning and linear classification Touché can reliably quantify much richer interactions; such as hand/or body postures, and how the touch interaction occurred, all with a single electrode.

So Hows it Work? - the details

A periodic electrical signal is injected into an electrode forming an oscillating electrical field. As the user’s hand approaches the electrode, a weak capacitive link is formed between the electrode and conductive physiological fluids inside the human hand, altering the signal supplied by the electrode. This happens because the user body introduces an additional path for flow of charges, acting as a charge “sink”. By measuring the degree of this signal change, touch events can be detected

So Hows it Work? - Implementation

So Hows it Work? - Implementation..details

Touché implements SFCS on a compact custom-built board powered by an ARM Cortex-M3 microprocessor The on-board signal generator excites an electrode with sinusoid sweeps and measures returned signal at each frequency. The resulting sampled signal is a capacitive profile of the touch interaction Finally, the capacitive profile is sent to a conventional computer over Bluetooth for classification. Recognized gestures can then be used to trigger different interactive functions.

Okay...So what are it's limitations?

Okay...So what are it's limitations?

  • Swept Frequency Capacitance Sensing requires high frequencies >3Mhz and the condition circuitry is for high frequency signals is a complex problem.
  • A computer is required for the identifications of interactions and the machine learning bits.
  • Touché is sensitive to small variations of capacitance.
  • "Walk-up" accuracy for body sensing is low as a result of different physiological body makeup
